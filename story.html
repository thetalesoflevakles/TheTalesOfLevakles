Carther is afraid of the ground.

But when you realize that everything that lives there is designed to capture, torture, or kill you, it's easy to understand why.

Carther lives thousands of feet up in the air. No, seriously, Carther and the rest of human society live in Helios, a city that floats in the sky. They have to. Unless they want to experience an excruciating death at the hands of criminals, rejectionists, or robotic beasts programmed to locate, hunt, and kill humans on the ground's surface, of course. It's definitely scary down there, but it wasn't always this way...

So then what happened? Honestly, nobody really knows perfectly, but the general consensus is that it's all Levakles' fault. He's the one that invented the technology that went rogue, after all. And like the rest of society, Carther was always curious as to what happened to Levakles after The Glitch anyways? At least, that was until really strange things started happening to Carther in his own beloved home.

Now, Carther and his friends face the perilous task to uncover the truth and prevent immenent tragedy. Of course, things are never as simple as they seem. Carther must learn firsthand what it will take to stop evil from fully eradicating what's left of humanity before it's too late. The Tales of Levakles awaits. Will the earth ever be safe for humanity again?
